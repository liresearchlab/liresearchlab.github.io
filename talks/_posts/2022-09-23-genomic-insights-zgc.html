SARS-CoV-2 emergence

Jan 11: First five genomes from Wuhan showed a novel SARS-like coronavirus

Jan 19: First 12 genomes from Wuhan and Bangkok showed lack of genetic diversity

Data from CAMS, China CDC, Fudan University, Hubei CDC, Thai MOPH, WIV; Figure from nextstrain.org

Single introduction into the human population between Nov 15 and Dec 15 and subsequent rapid human-to-human spread

Bedford et al. Jan 23, 2020. Technical Report.

Rapid global pandemic spread during spring 2020

Epidemic in the USA was introduced from China in late Jan and from Europe during Feb

nextstrain.org

Seattle Flu Study detects local circulation and charts early epidemic in Washington State

Bedford et al. 2020. Science.

Direct introduction from China ~Feb 1 responsible for the majority of the epidemic

BA.4 and BA.5 emerged from South Africa with spike mutations 452R and 486V

BA.4 / BA.5 described in Tegally et al. 2022. Nat Med. Figure from nextstrain.org

Population genetic expectation of variant frequency under selection

$x' = \frac{x \, (1+s)}{x \, (1+s) + (1-x)}$ for frequency $x$ over one generation with selective advantage $s$

$x(t) = \frac{x_0 \, (1+s)^t}{x_0 \, (1+s)^t + (1-x_0)}$ for initial frequency $x_0$ over $t$ generations

Trajectories are linear once logit transformed via $\mathrm{log}(\frac{x}{1 - x})$

Consistent fitness advantage of BA.5 across countries

BA.2.75 shows selective advantage over BA.5

Sequences generated and shared at an unprecedented pace with >5M in 2022 (compare to 8k flu genomes in 2022)

Data from gisaid.org

More broadly, I expect continued genomic surveillance to respond to ongoing evolution of SARS-CoV-2, but that capacity generated for pandemic response will pivot to emerging and endemic pathogens according to threat / burden
